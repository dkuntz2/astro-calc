Dealing With Film

When shooting long exposures with film, you need to deal with reciprocity failure (the relationship between shutter speed and exposure breaking down). Thankfully, Ilford publishes what those values are for their black and white films, which means we can calculate the correct shutter speed.

As a general note, film loves getting light, so overexposing is generally not an issue. As such, you probably can't go wrong with just leaving the shutter open forever. (okay, forever is probably too long)

This is not true for digital sensors. If you overexpose by too much on a digital camera you will lose all detail in the affected areas.

Technical Notes

This calculator assumes you're working with an EV of -10. -10 was selected because it's a good working value for capturing the galactic center.

The equations used for determine shutter speeds are based on those in the Wikipedia Exposure Value entry.

Determining the relative EV based on ISO speed:

EVrelative = EVbase + log2(ISO/100)
Which ultimately translates to
EV = -10 + log2(ISO/100)

Determining the shutter speed for a given EV and f-stop:

EV = log2(f2 / S)
Which is reduced to
S = f2 / 2EV
